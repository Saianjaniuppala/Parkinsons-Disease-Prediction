Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that affects the central nervous system. It is characterized by the progressive loss of dopamine-producing brain cells, leading to various motor and non-motor symptoms. The main problem statement for Parkinson's disease can be framed as follows: develop effective strategies for early detection, accurate diagnosis, and personalized treatment of Parkinson's disease to improve the quality of life for patients, slow down disease progression, and eventually find a cure for this debilitating neurological condition.
